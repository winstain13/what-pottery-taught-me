# Why pottery?

- It looks calm, but it is full of surprises
- Every stage changes the piece
- The final result is never fully visible at the beginning
- That uncertainty is the point, not the problem

# The stages of pottery

1. **Forming**
2. **Trimming**
3. **Bisque fire**
4. **Glazing**
5. **Glaze fire**
6. **Buffing**

::right::

# The lesson underneath

At every stage, you guide the piece — but you do not fully control it.

That is what makes pottery beautiful.

---

# 1. Forming

### This is the most hopeful stage

- The clay is soft
- The possibilities feel wide open
- You can imagine exactly what the piece will become

<div class="mt-8 rounded-xl bg-black/10 p-4">
But at this stage, your vision is still mostly a story.
</div>

---

# 2. Trimming

### Pottery gets better when you remove what doesn't belong

- You refine the shape
- You make it lighter, cleaner, more intentional
- You realize the first version was never the final version

<div class="mt-8 text-lg italic opacity-80">
Sometimes making something beautiful means cutting away, not adding more.
</div>

---

# 3. Bisque fire

### The first real surrender

- Heat changes everything
- The piece becomes more durable
- Small assumptions get exposed

<div class="mt-8 rounded-xl border border-white/20 p-4">
Once it enters the kiln, part of the outcome belongs to the elements.
</div>

---

# 4. Glazing

### You choose, but you still don't know

- You pick colors and finishes
- You layer intention onto the surface
- You make your best guess about what it will become

<div class="mt-8">
Glaze is a promise, not a guarantee.
</div>

---

# 5. Glaze fire

### The transformation you can't fully watch

- Heat, chemistry, timing, and atmosphere all matter
- The kiln collaborates whether you want it to or not
- The finished piece can exceed your plan — or ignore it

<div class="mt-8 text-lg font-semibold">
This might be my favorite part: the reveal.
</div>

---

# 6. Buffing

### The final care

- Small finishing touches matter
- You slow down enough to really see the piece
- By now, it is no longer the thing you first imagined

<div class="mt-8 italic opacity-80">
And that is usually a good thing.
</div>

# What pottery taught me

## Enjoy the unexpected

- Control is always partial
- The process is part collaboration, part surrender
- Surprise is not failure
- The final form carries more than the original plan

# Pottery and life feel similar to me

| Pottery | Life |
| --- | --- |
| You begin with intention | You begin with a plan |
| The process changes the outcome | Experience changes you |
| The elements have their say | The world has its say |
| The result surprises you | So does growth |
